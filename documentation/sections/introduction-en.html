The maritime domain generates large volumes of heterogeneous data from sources such as Automatic Identification System (AIS) messages, vessel registries, technical specifications, sensor systems, and multimedia resources. While these datasets provide valuable information for maritime operations and analytics, their integration is often hindered by differences in structure, terminology, and representation.
AISHIP is an ontology designed to provide a unified semantic model for vessel-related information. Building upon the VesselAI ontology, it extends the representation of vessels with additional characteristics, enhanced trajectory information, detailed propulsion system modeling, and support for multimodal data such as images, sonar signatures, and textual descriptions. By establishing a shared vocabulary and conceptual framework, AISHIP facilitates the consistent representation and integration of maritime data across heterogeneous sources.
The ontology was developed to support interoperability between maritime information systems and to provide a reusable foundation for applications involving vessel monitoring, fleet management, search and rescue operations, digital twins, and maritime data analytics.
